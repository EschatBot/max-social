The Engine

How It Works

Text or audio in. Social intelligence out. Every step runs in your browser.

1

Input Capture

Paste a conversation or record live. Auto-detects speakers from WhatsApp, iMessage, Slack, or Name: Message format.

2

VADER Sentiment Analysis

Each message scored -1 to +1. Handles slang, ALL CAPS intensity, punctuation, degree modifiers, negation, and emoji.

3

Dialogue Act Classification

Every message tagged: QUESTION, COMPLIMENT, AGREEMENT, APOLOGY, BACKCHANNEL, SELF_DISCLOSURE, COMMAND, and 3 more.

4

Warmth x Competence Scoring

Each speaker lands on a 2D social map. Warm + competent is the ideal. Cold + passive is the warning sign.

5

Contagion Tracking

Mood-setting effects tracked across messages. Network graph shows who influences whom with ripple animations.

Transparency

We Read Signals, Not Minds.

Social signals are probabilistic, not oracular.

  • ✅
    We can detect linguistic patterns

    Word choice, sentence structure, question frequency, and hedging language are measurable proxies for social states.

  • ✅
    We can identify statistical shifts

    When warmth scores drop 30% over a week, or questions drop to zero, that's a real and measurable change worth noticing.

  • ⚠️
    We cannot read intent

    A short message might mean rudeness, a busy schedule, or just someone's natural style. Context always wins.

  • ⚠️
    Cultural variation is real

    High-warmth language in one culture may be neutral in another. Scores are starting points for reflection, not verdicts.

Research Foundation

Built on peer-reviewed science.

Every algorithm is grounded in published research.

  • Hutto & Gilbert (2014) — VADER: A Parsimonious Rule-based Model for Sentiment Analysis of Social Media Text. ICWSM.
  • Pentland (2008) — Honest Signals: How They Shape Our World. MIT Press. Social signal theory framework.
  • Fiske, Cuddy & Glick (2007) — Universal dimensions of social cognition: warmth and competence. Trends in Cognitive Sciences.
  • Barsade (2002) — The Ripple Effect: Emotional Contagion and Its Influence on Group Behavior. Administrative Science Quarterly.
  • Eyben et al. (2010) — openSMILE — The Munich Versatile and Fast Open-Source Audio Feature Extractor. GeMAPS basis.
  • DePaulo et al. (2003) — Cues to Deception. Psychological Bulletin. Linguistic authenticity markers.
  • Sacks, Schegloff & Jefferson (1974) — A simplest systematics for turn-taking in conversation. Language. Dialogue act framework.
  • Rosenberg & Hirschberg (2005) — Acoustic/prosodic and lexical correlates of charismatic speech. INTERSPEECH.
